avś_20.035.01.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ प्र॒ तव॒से तुरा॒य प्र॒यो न॒ हर्मि स्तो॒मं मा॒हिनाय ।
ऋ॒चीषमाया॒ध्रिगव ओ॒हम् इ॒न्द्राय ब्र॒ह्माणि रात॒तमा ॥१॥

asmā́ íd u prá taváse turā́ya práyo ná harmi stómaṃ mā́hināya |
ŕ̥cīṣamāyā́dhrigava óham índrāya bráhmāṇi rātátamā ||1||

To him, to him swift, strong, and high-exalted, I bring my song
of praise as dainty viands;
My thought to him resistless, meet for praises, prayers offered
most devotedly to Indra.

avś_20.035.02.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ प्र॒य इव प्र॒ यंसि भ॒राम्य् आन्गूषं॒ बा॒धे सुवृक्ति॒ ।
इ॒न्द्राय हृदा॒ म॒नसा मनीषा॒ प्रत्ना॒य प॒त्ये धि॒यो मर्जयन्त ॥२॥

asmā́ íd u práya iva prá yaṃsi bhárāmy āngūṣáṃ bā́dhe suvr̥ktí |
índrāya hr̥dā́ mánasā manīṣā́ pratnā́ya pátye dhíyo marjayanta ||2||

To him I offer praise as choice refreshment, bring forth my song,
with seemly laud besiege him.
For Indra, Lord of olden time, the singers shall deck their hymns
with heart and mind and spirit.

avś_20.035.03.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ त्य॒म् उपमं॒ स्वर्षां॒ भ॒राम्य् आङ्गूष॒म् आस्ये॑न ।
मं॒हिष्ठम् अ॒छोक्तिभिर् मतीनां॒ सुवृक्ति॒भिः सूरिं॒ वावृध॒ध्यै ॥३॥

asmā́ íd u tyám upamáṃ svarṣā́ṃ bhárāmy āṅgūṣám āsyèna |
máṃhiṣṭham áchoktibhir matīnā́ṃ suvr̥ktíbhiḥ sūríṃ vāvr̥dhádhyai ||3||

To him then with my lips my song of praises, excellent, winning
heavenly light, I offer,
To magnify with hymns of invocation and eulogies the Lord,
most bounteous Giver.

avś_20.035.04.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ स्तो॒मं सं॒ हिनोमि र॒थं न॒ त॒ष्टेव त॒त्सिनाय ।
गि॒रश् च गि॒र्वाहसे सुवृक्ती॒न्द्राय विश्वमिन्वं॒ मे॒धिराय ॥४॥

asmā́ íd u stómaṃ sáṃ hinomi ráthaṃ ná táṣṭeva tátsināya |
gíraś ca gírvāhase suvr̥ktī́ndrāya viśvaminváṃ médhirāya ||4||

Even for him I frame a laud—so fashions the wright a chariot
for the man who needs it
Songs for wise Indra hymned with invocation, a song composed
with care and all-impelling.

avś_20.035.05.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ स॒प्तिम् इव श्रवस्ये॒न्द्रायार्कं॒ जुह्वा॑ स॒म् अञ्जे ।
वीर॒म् दानौ॒कसं वन्द॒ध्यै पुरां॒ गूर्त॒श्रवसं दर्मा॒णम् ॥५॥

asmā́ íd u sáptim iva śravasyéndrāyārkáṃ juhvā̀ sám añje |
vīrám dānáukasaṃ vandádhyai purā́ṃ gūrtáśravasaṃ darmā́ṇam ||5||

So with my tongue I deck, to please that Indra, my hymn as't-
were a horse, through love of glory,
To reverence the Hero, bounteous Giver, famed far and wide,
destroyer of the castles.

avś_20.035.06.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ त्व॒ष्टा तक्षद् व॒ज्रं स्व॒पस्तमं स्वर्यं॑ र॒णाय ।
वृत्र॒स्य चिद् विद॒द् ये॒न म॒र्म तुज॒न्न् ई॒शानस् तुजता॒ कियेधाः॒ ॥६॥

asmā́ íd u tváṣṭā takṣad vájraṃ svápastamaṃ svaryàṃ ráṇāya |
vr̥trásya cid vidád yéna márma tujánn ī́śānas tujatā́ kiyedhā́ḥ ||6||

Even for him hath Tvashtar forged the thunder, most deftly
wrought, celestial, for the battle.
Wherewith he reached the vital parts of Vritra, striking—the
vast, the mighty—with the striker,

avś_20.035.07.

अस्ये॒द् उ मातुः॒ स॒वनेषु सद्यो॒ महः॒ पितुं॒ पपिवां॒ चा॒र्व् अ॒न्ना ।
मुषाय॒द् वि॒ष्णुः पचतं॒ स॒हीयान् वि॒ध्यद् वराहं॒ तिरो॒ अ॒द्रिम् अ॒स्ता ॥७॥

asyéd u mātúḥ sávaneṣu sadyó maháḥ pitúṃ papivā́ṃ cā́rv ánnā |
muṣāyád víṣṇuḥ pacatáṃ sáhīyān vídhyad varāháṃ tiró ádrim ástā ||7||

As soon as, at libations of his mother, great Vishnu had drunk
up the draught, he plundered.
The dainty cates, the cooked mess; but One stronger transfixed
the wild boar, shooting through the mountain.

avś_20.035.08.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ ग्ना॒श् चिद् देव॒पत्नीर् इ॒न्द्रायार्क॒म् अहिह॒त्य ऊवुः ।
प॒रि द्या॒वापृथिवी॒ जभ्र उर्वी॒ ना॒स्य ते॒ महिमा॒नं प॒रि ष्टः ॥८॥

asmā́ íd u gnā́ś cid devápatnīr índrāyārkám ahihátya ūvuḥ |
pári dyā́vāpr̥thivī́ jabhra urvī́ nā́sya té mahimā́naṃ pári ṣṭaḥ ||8||

To him, to Indra when he slew the Dragon, the Dames too,
Consorts of the Gods, wove praises.
The mighty heaven and earth hath he encompassed: thy great-
ness heaven and earth, combined, exceed not.

avś_20.035.09.

अस्ये॒द् एव॒ प्र॒ रिरिचे महित्वं॒ दिव॒स् पृथिव्याः॒ प॒र्य् अन्त॒रिक्षात् ।
स्वरा॒ल् इ॒न्द्रो द॒म आ॒ विश्व॒गूर्तः स्वरि॒र् अ॒मत्रो ववक्षे र॒णाय ॥९॥

asyéd evá prá ririce mahitváṃ divás pr̥thivyā́ḥ páry antárikṣāt |
svarā́l índro dáma ā́ viśvágūrtaḥ svarír ámatro vavakṣe ráṇāya ||9||

Yea, of a truth, his magnitude surpasseth the magnitude of
earth, mid-air and heaven.
Indra whom all men praise, the Sovran Ruler, waxed in his
home loud-voiced and strong for battle.

avś_20.035.10.

अस्ये॒द् एव॒ श॒वसा शुश॒न्तं वि॒ वृश्चद् व॒ज्रेण वृत्र॒म् इ॒न्द्रः ।
गा॒ न॒ व्राणा॒ अव॒नीर् अमुञ्चद् अभि॒ श्र॒वो दाव॒ने स॒चेताः ॥१०॥

asyéd evá śávasā śuśántaṃ ví vr̥ścad vájreṇa vr̥trám índraḥ |
gā́ ná vrāṇā́ avánīr amuñcad abhí śrávo dāváne sácetāḥ ||10||

Through his own strength with bolt of thunder Indra smote
piece-meal Vritra, drier up of waters.
He let the floods go free, like cows imprisoned, for glory, with
a heart inclined to bounty.

avś_20.035.11.

अस्ये॒द् उ त्वेष॒सा रन्त सि॒न्धवः प॒रि य॒द् व॒ज्रेण सीम् अ॒यछत् ।
ईशानकृ॒द् दाशु॒षे दशस्य॒न् तुर्वी॒तये गाधं॒ तुर्व॒णिः कः॒ ॥११॥

asyéd u tveṣásā ranta síndhavaḥ pári yád vájreṇa sīm áyachat |
īśānakŕ̥d dāśúṣe daśasyán turvī́taye gādháṃ turváṇiḥ káḥ ||11||

Through his resplendent power still stood the rivers when with
his bolt on every side he stayed them.
With lordly might favouring him who worshipped, he made a
ford, victorious, for Turviti.

avś_20.035.12.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ प्र॒ भरा तू॒तुजानो वृत्रा॒य व॒ज्रम् ई॒शानः कियेधाः॒ ।
गो॒र् न॒ प॒र्व वि॒ रदा तिरश्चे॒ष्यन्न् अ॒र्णांस्य् अपां॒ चर॒ध्यै ॥१२॥

asmā́ íd u prá bharā tū́tujāno vr̥trā́ya vájram ī́śānaḥ kiyedhā́ḥ |
gór ná párva ví radā tiraścéṣyann árṇāṃsy apā́ṃ carádhyai ||12||

Vast, with thine ample power, with eager movement against this
Vritra cast thy bolt of thunder.
Rend thou his joints, as of an ox dissevered, with bolt oblique
that floods of rain may follow.

avś_20.035.13.

अस्ये॒द् उ प्र॒ ब्रूहि पूर्व्या॒णि तुर॒स्य क॒र्माणि न॒व्य उक्थैः॒ ।
युधे॒ य॒द् इष्णान॒ आ॒युधान्य् ऋघाय॒माणो निरिणा॒ति श॒त्रून् ॥१३॥

asyéd u prá brūhi pūrvyā́ṇi turásya kármāṇi návya uktháiḥ |
yudhé yád iṣṇāná ā́yudhāny r̥ghāyámāṇo niriṇā́ti śátrūn ||13||

Sing with new lauds his exploits wrought aforetime, the deeds
of him, yea, him who moveth swiftly,
When, hurling forth his weapons in the battle, he with impetuous
wrath lays low the foemen.

avś_20.035.14.

अस्ये॒द् उ भिया॒ गिर॒यश् च दृल्हा॒ द्या॒वा च भू॒मा जनु॒षस् तुजेते ।
उ॒पो वेन॒स्य जो॒गुवान ओणिं॒ सद्यो॒ भुवद् वीर्या॑य नोधाः॒ ॥१४॥

asyéd u bhiyā́ giráyaś ca dr̥lhā́ dyā́vā ca bhū́mā janúṣas tujete |
úpo venásya jóguvāna oṇíṃ sadyó bhuvad vīryā̀ya nodhā́ḥ ||14||

When he, yea, he is born the firm-set mountains and the whole
heaven and earth tremble in terror.
May Nodhas ever lauding the protection of this dear Friend win
straightway strength heroic.

avś_20.035.15.

अस्मा॒ इ॒द् उ त्य॒द् अ॒नु दाय्य् एषाम् ए॒को य॒द् वव्ने॒ भू॒रेर् ई॒शानः ।
प्रै॒तशं सू॒र्ये पस्पृधानं॒ सौ॒वश्व्ये सु॒ष्विम् आवद् इ॒न्द्रः ॥१५॥

asmā́ íd u tyád ánu dāyy eṣām éko yád vavné bhū́rer ī́śānaḥ |
práitaśaṃ sū́rye paspr̥dhānáṃ sáuvaśvye súṣvim āvad índraḥ ||15||

Now unto him of these things hath been given what he, who
rules alone o'er much, electeth.
Indra helped Etasa, the Soma presser, contending in the chariot-
race with Sūrya.

avś_20.035.16.

एवा॒ ते हारियोजना सुवृक्ती॒न्द्र ब्र॒ह्माणि गो॒तमासो अक्रन् ।
ऐ॒षु विश्व॒पेशसं धि॒यं धाः प्रात॒र् मक्षू॒ धिया॒वसुर् जगम्यात् ॥१६॥

evā́ te hāriyojanā suvr̥ktī́ndra bráhmāṇi gótamāso akran |
áiṣu viśvápeśasaṃ dhíyaṃ dhāḥ prātár makṣū́ dhiyā́vasur jagamyāt ||16||

Thus to thee, Indra, yoker of bay coursers, the Gotamas have
brought their prayers and praises.
Bestow upon them thought, decked with all beauty. May he,
enriched with prayer, come soon and early.
