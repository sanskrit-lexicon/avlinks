avś_20.125.01.

अ॒पेन्द्र प्रा॒चो मघवन्न् अमि॒त्रान् अ॒पा॒पाचो अभिभूते नुदस्व ।
अ॒पो॒दीचो अ॒प शूराधरा॒च उरौ॒ य॒था त॒व श॒र्मन् म॒देम ॥१॥

ápendra prā́co maghavann amítrān ápā́pāco abhibhūte nudasva |
ápódīco ápa śūrādharā́ca uráu yáthā táva śárman mádema ||1||

Drive all our enemies away, O Indra, the western, mighty Con-
queror, and the eastern,
Hero, drive off our northern foes and southern, that we in thy
wide shelter may be joyful.

avś_20.125.02.

कुवि॒द् अङ्ग॒ य॒वमन्तो य॒वं चिद् य॒था दा॒न्त्य् अनुपूर्वं॒ वियू॒य ।
इहे॒हैषां कृणुहि भो॒जनानि ये॒ बर्हि॒षो न॒मोवृक्तिं न॒ जग्मुः॒ ॥२॥

kuvíd aṅgá yávamanto yávaṃ cid yáthā dā́nty anupūrváṃ viyū́ya |
ihéhaiṣāṃ kr̥ṇuhi bhójanāni yé barhíṣo námovr̥ktiṃ ná jagmúḥ ||2||

What then? As men whose fields are full of barley reap the ripe
corn removing it in order,
So bring the food of those men, bring it hither, who come not
to prepare the grass for worship.

avś_20.125.03.

नहि॒ स्थू॒र्य् ऋतुथा॒ यात॒म् अस्ति नो॒त॒ श्र॒वो विविदे संगमे॒षु ।
गव्य॒न्त इ॒न्द्रं सख्या॒य वि॒प्रा अश्वाय॒न्तो वृ॒षणं वाज॒यन्तः ॥३॥

nahí sthū́ry r̥tuthā́ yātám asti nótá śrávo vivide saṃgaméṣu |
gavyánta índraṃ sakhyā́ya víprā aśvāyánto vŕ̥ṣaṇaṃ vājáyantaḥ ||3||

Men come not with one horse at sacred seasons; thus they
obtain no honour in assemblies.
Sages desiring herds of kine and horses strengthen the mighty
Indra for his friendship.

avś_20.125.04.

युवं॒ सुरा॒मम् अश्विना न॒मुचाव् आसुरे॒ स॒चा ।
विपिपाना॒ शुभस् पती इ॒न्द्रं क॒र्मस्व् आवतम् ॥४॥

yuváṃ surā́mam aśvinā námucāv āsuré sácā |
vipipānā́ śubhas patī índraṃ kármasv āvatam ||4||

Ye, Asvins, Lords of Splendour, drank full draughts of grateful
Soma juice,
And aided Indra in his work with Namuchi of Asura birth.

avś_20.125.05.

पुत्र॒म् इव पित॒राव् अश्वि॒नोभे॒न्द्राव॒थुः का॒व्यैर् दंस॒नाभिः ।
य॒त् सुरा॒मं व्य् अ॒पिबः श॒चीभिः स॒रस्वती त्वा मघवन्न् अभिष्णक् ॥५॥

putrám iva pitárāv aśvínobhéndrāváthuḥ kā́vyair daṃsánābhiḥ |
yát surā́maṃ vy ápibaḥ śácībhiḥ sárasvatī tvā maghavann abhiṣṇak ||5||

As parents aid a son, both Asvins, Indra, aided thee with their
wondrous powers and wisdom
When thou, with might, hadst drunk the draught that glad-
dens, Sarasvati, O Maghavan refreshed thee.

avś_20.125.06.

इ॒न्द्रः सुत्रा॒मा स्व॒वां अ॒वोभिः सुमृडीको॒ भवतु विश्व॒वेदाः ।
बा॒धतां द्वे॒षो अ॒भयं नः कृणोतु सुवी॒र्यस्य प॒तयः स्याम ॥६॥

índraḥ sutrā́mā svávām̐ ávobhiḥ sumr̥ḍīkó bhavatu viśvávedāḥ |
bā́dhatāṃ dvéṣo ábhayaṃ naḥ kr̥ṇotu suvī́ryasya pátayaḥ syāma ||6||

Indra is strong to save, rich in assistance: may he, possessing
all, be kind and gracious.
May he disperse our foes and give us safety, and may we be the
lords of hero vigour.

avś_20.125.07.

स॒ सुत्रा॒मा स्व॒वां इ॒न्द्रो अस्म॒द् आरा॒च् चिद् द्वे॒षः सनुत॒र् युयोतु ।
त॒स्य वयं॒ सुमतौ॒ यज्ञि॒यस्या॒पि भद्रे॒ सौमनसे॒ स्याम ॥७॥

sá sutrā́mā svávām̐ índro asmád ārā́c cid dvéṣaḥ sanutár yuyotu |
tásya vayáṃ sumatáu yajñíyasyā́pi bhadré saumanasé syāma ||7||

May we enjoy his favour, his the holy: may we enjoy his blessed
loving-kindness.
May this rich Indra, as our good protector, drive off and keep
afar all those who hate us.
